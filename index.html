Haili Copas-Starke

Technology can sometimes be tricky.

But that's what I'm here for -- I've given myself lots of headaches so you can spare yourself the trouble.

Haili is (currently) a Bay-Area start-up and political consultant who specializes in all things growth.

A Nebraska native, Haili became passionate about politics and advocacy efforts while attending the University of Nebraska at Omaha where she served as the Director of the Women's Resource Center and the Office Assistant of the Women's and Gender Studies program. After working for people like Senator Ben Nelson and City Councilman Garry Gernandt, she moved to New York City to be apply her efforts there. While in NYC, Haili co-founded Women of Color for Progress, a nonprofit that empowers women of color to excel, lead, and be heard.

During her career in politics, Haili noticed the lack of technological innovation in politics and how that related to the gender pay gap so she shifted her focus on all things technology.
